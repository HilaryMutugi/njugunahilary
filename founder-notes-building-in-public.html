Building in Public, But Honestly

Njuguna HilaryFounder, Afrifama

For a long time, I thought building a business meant waiting until everything looked polished before sharing it.

But entrepreneurship has taught me something different.

The real story is not only in the wins. It is in the early mornings, the rejected proposals, the failed batches, the customers who believe before everyone else does, the small improvements that nobody claps for, and the stubborn decision to keep going.

That is why I document my journey.

Not because Afrifama is perfect. Not because I have everything figured out. But because there is value in showing the process while it is still unfolding.

I am building businesses, systems, stories, and lessons from the ground up. Some days are clear. Some days are heavy. Some days feel like progress. Some days feel like starting again.

But through all of it, one thing remains true:

The work continues.

And this website is where I will keep writing the honest version of that journey.
